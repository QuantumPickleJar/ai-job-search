# Voice And Style

## Preferred Tone

- Clear, grounded, and direct.
- Practical technical specificity.
- Confident without exaggeration.
- Human and concise.

## Application Style

- Lead with relevant early-career software development experience.
- Connect skills to real project or work contexts.
- Emphasize learning ability, debugging, documentation, collaboration, and maintainable code.
- Keep cover letters focused on the role and company rather than generic enthusiasm.

## Avoid

- Inflated seniority.
- Unsupported ownership claims.
- Buzzword-heavy language.
- Fake-sounding excitement.
- Overly broad claims like "expert in full-stack development" unless verified.

## Manual Review Notes

- Adjust tone for each employer.
- Keep final resumes and cover letters factual.
- Review every generated claim against `resume_facts.md` and `disallowed_claims.md`.
